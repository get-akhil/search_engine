Internet of Things (IoT)

1. INTRODUCTION
The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical devices, vehicles, appliances, and sensors connected to the internet, capable of collecting, exchanging, and processing data.
IoT integrates electronics, communication, and computing technologies to create smart and automated systems.

2. HISTORY & EVOLUTION

1980s: Early embedded sensors in machines.

1990s: Concept of networked devices and RFID-based connectivity.

2000s: Expansion of internet and wireless communication enabled IoT growth.

Present: IoT applications in smart homes, industries, healthcare, and transportation.
