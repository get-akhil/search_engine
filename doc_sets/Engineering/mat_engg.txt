Materials Science Engineering
1. INTRODUCTION
Materials Science Engineering is the branch that studies the structure, properties, and applications of materials like metals, ceramics, polymers, and composites.
It combines principles of physics, chemistry, and engineering to design new materials that meet specific requirements in industries such as aerospace, automotive, electronics, and healthcare.

2. HISTORY & EVOLUTION

Ancient Era: Use of natural materials like stone, metals, and wood.

19th Century: Industrial Revolution led to large-scale metallurgy and material processing.

20th Century: Polymers, composites, and semiconductors emerged.

21st Century: Nanotechnology, smart materials, and biomaterials are transforming industries.

3. MAJOR SUBFIELDS

Metallurgy: Study of metals and alloys.

Polymer Engineering: Design and synthesis of plastics and polymers.

Ceramics & Composites: Advanced structural materials.

Nanomaterials: Materials at the nanoscale with unique properties.

Biomaterials: Materials for medical implants and devices.
