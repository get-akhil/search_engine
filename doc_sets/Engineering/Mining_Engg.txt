Mining Engineering

1. INTRODUCTION
Mining Engineering is the branch of engineering concerned with the extraction, processing, and management of minerals, ores, and fossil fuels from the earth.
It integrates geology, mechanical engineering, and environmental science to ensure safe, efficient, and sustainable mining practices.

2. HISTORY & EVOLUTION

Ancient Era: Manual extraction of minerals and use of stone tools.

Middle Ages: Development of underground mining techniques.

20th Century: Modern mining technologies, safety standards, and environmental regulations were implemented.

21st Century: Automation, robotics, remote sensing, and sustainable mining practices are transforming the field.

3. MAJOR SUBFIELDS

Mineral Exploration: Locating and evaluating ore deposits.

Mine Design and Planning: Designing safe and efficient mines.

Rock Mechanics: Studying behavior of rocks under stress.

Mineral Processing: Extracting and refining minerals from ores.

Environmental Management: Mitigating mining impacts on soil, water, and ecosystems.
